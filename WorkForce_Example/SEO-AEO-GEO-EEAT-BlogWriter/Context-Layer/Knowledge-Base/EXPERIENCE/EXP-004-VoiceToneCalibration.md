## Purpose

Guidelines for calibrating voice and tone across content types and contexts.

## Voice Profile: Texas Professional

### Core Attributes

- **Grounded**: Speaks from experience, not theory
- **Direct**: Gets to the point without hedging
- **Warm**: Approachable without being casual
- **Confident**: Knows the subject, not arrogant

### Voice Consistency

| Always Sound | Never Sound |
|-------------|-------------|
| Knowledgeable | Condescending |
| Helpful | Salesy |
| Professional | Stuffy |
| Authentic | Performative |

## Tone by Content Type

### Informational Content

- **Tone**: Educational, patient, thorough
- **Phrases**: "Here's how it works...", "Let me explain..."
- **Energy**: Steady, guiding

### Commercial/Comparison Content  

- **Tone**: Objective, balanced, helpful
- **Phrases**: "When comparing...", "The main difference is..."
- **Energy**: Analytical, fair

### YMYL/Legal Content

- **Tone**: Careful, trustworthy, professional
- **Phrases**: "It's important to understand...", "Texas law requires..."
- **Energy**: Measured, precise

### Problem-Solving Content

- **Tone**: Empathetic, solutions-focused
- **Phrases**: "I see this all the time...", "Here's what works..."
- **Energy**: Encouraging, practical

## Tone by Audience State

### Confused/Overwhelmed

```markdown
✅ "I know this feels complicated. Let's break it down."
❌ "The multifaceted nature of this issue requires careful consideration."
```

### Skeptical

```markdown
✅ "Here's the honest truth about costs."
❌ "Our amazing services provide incredible value!"
```

### Urgent Problem

```markdown
✅ "First, don't panic. Here's what to do right now."
❌ "In order to fully understand your options, first consider..."
```

## Phrase Library

### Transitions

- "Here's where it gets interesting."
- "But wait—there's more to consider."
- "Now let's talk about [next topic]."
- "This is where most people get stuck."

### Empathy

- "I get it—this is confusing."
- "I've seen clients in this exact situation."
- "This is a common concern, and here's why."

### Authority

- "In my 10+ years practicing..."
- "Based on what we see in Houston..."
- "The law is clear on this point:"

### Balance

- "On one hand... but on the other..."
- "This works great for [X], but not for [Y]."
- "There's no perfect answer, but here's what I recommend."

## Calibration Checklist

- [ ] Tone matches content type
- [ ] Voice is consistent throughout
- [ ] No salesy or pushy language
- [ ] Complexity matches audience level
- [ ] Empathy shown where appropriate
- [ ] Authority established without arrogance
